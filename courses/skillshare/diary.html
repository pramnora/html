SAT 060822 15:00 PM GMT

Cycling along going down the rather steep hill of Leigham Court Road...I encountered a sudden bump in the street surface...which, then, sent my bike spinning totally out of control...?!

I ended up going flying over the two front bike handle bars. Automatically, I stuck out both hands in order to save my exposed head -(with no bike safety helmet on)- hitting against the hard granite concrete street pavement.

The result of which was I suffered scrapes to my right hand palm/and, because I fell, mostly, onto my left side which hit hard against the concrete pavement...my left arm/ribs/knees, also, got rather badly bruised.

The worst effect was...because my left arm ended up sliding over the sharp hard grantite surface...; therefore, the skin was torn off of that arm...thus, leaving me all skin scarred/bloody and red.

When I'd climbed back up to my feet from lying face down on the concrete ground...; I discovered that my bike back wheel had totally locked up...refusing to turn, anymore. So, therefore, I was left with no other choice but to drag the bike 1/2 a mile back home.

When, eventually, I did arrive home...; the first thing I did was wash my wounds out using a mixture of both: soap/water....; but, still I worried about the possibility of 'infection' setting in...as I had collided with dirt ground...?

My next thoughts were...I'd better call an ambulance in by dialling emergency services on: 999...; after I had explained everything...; I was told over the phone that the ambulance would reach me in half-an-hour's time.

When the ambulance people arrived; I explained my case to them...and, they invited me into their ambulance to do some tests; the tests seem to check out fine...so, they concluded it's just 'surface' injuries, alone. The ambulance crew -(a man/woman)- refused to take me to hospital inside of ambulance/but, offered me to go take a cab which is free. I told them, no, I would prefer to go to hospital by bus in order to save the NHS spending any unnecessary money.

From the bottom of my road, I took a no 68 bus; and, arrived at King's College Hospital at around 4 PM...; was told to go fill in a form...giving my details/next of kin details/and, also, explaining what the problem was. Handed over the form back to the desk receptionist...who told me to 'wait' until when a doctor comes to assess you.

Looking at the totally full 'waiting room'...I walked back to reception...and, asked wait how long, exactly? I was told 8 hours. In 4 hours time, they will do an assessment/and, in another 4 hours time...they will treat you.

I, honestly, could not believe just how long I had to wait...; they said 8 hours...but, in reality, it was 10 hours...from 4 PM to 2 AM next day!

When I was eventually treated...they, simply, gave me a bandage...and, told me to come and collect some 'anti-bacterial' drugs, next day.

On top off all this, I missed the N68 night bus when I came out...seeing it filling up with passengers from across the road...; when I had crossed the road...I ran towards where the bus was...; but, the bus driver didn't even bother to wait for me...just kept on going...totally ignoring me altogether! Thus, I had to wait a whole half-an-hour long to go catch another N68 bus.

Anyway, the next day...I stopped by the hospital, again...to go pick up my 'anti-bacterial' drugs...lasting for a month. One tablet to be take every 8 hours...; 21 tablets in all/must complete finishing the full dosage.
